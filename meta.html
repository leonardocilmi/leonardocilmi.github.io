Background

I worked with Meta's enterprise products team to design and ship a suite of products with the goal of optimizing the company's $34 billion+ in annual spend, improving the everyday work experience of hundreds of thousands of Internal and external employees, and providing the gear 80,000+ Meta employees need to do their work.

Meta Procurement

I had the opportunity to help lead the redesign of Meta's procurement platform, buy@. buy@ hosts a wide range of tools that are integrated with Workplace and keep Meta and the companies using Workplace running. These tools include the enterprise store (where employees go to get the gear they need for work), purchasing, contracts, sourcing, approvals, case management, and more. The promise of the platform was to optimize Meta's spend by providing a centralized and tracked experience where employees can execute procurements and get the gear they need for work. Done right, buy@ had the opportunity to improve employee's work days, make meaningful impact with its spend, and save Meta billions. After the first year, adoption was low, cycle times and cost for procurement were unchanged, and customer satisfaction was below 2.5/5. That's where we stepped in. Over the course of 8 months we reimagined the platform from the ground up. The results spoke for themselves:

💸

Saved Meta 8% on orders, $4B+ annually

🌎

$300M+ increase in yearly meaningful spend

⏰

Shortened average order times by 4 days

👍

40% increase in customer satisfaction
